Why an Emergency Fund is Your Financial Safety Net

By Grady the Owl 🦉

Life throws curveballs — medical bills, job loss, car repairs. An emergency fund gives you a financial cushion so surprises don’t become disasters.

How Much Should You Save?

Most experts recommend at least 3 to 6 months of living expenses. Start small: even $500 can stop you from relying on high-interest credit cards.

“Don’t wait for the storm to hit before buying an umbrella.”

Why It Matters

  • 🚫 Avoids high-interest debt
  • 🛏️ Provides peace of mind
  • 💪 Builds financial resilience

How to Start

1. Open a separate savings account.
2. Automate small transfers each payday.
3. Treat it as untouchable — only for emergencies.
