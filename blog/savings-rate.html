What Is a Good Savings Rate?

Your savings rate is the percentage of your after-tax income that you set aside for the future. For example, if you earn $5,000 per month and save $1,000, your savings rate is 20%.

General Guidelines

  • 10% or less: Minimum, often not enough for early retirement.
  • 15–20%: Solid, puts you on track for a comfortable retirement at normal age.
  • 25–50%: Aggressive, often tied to FIRE (Financial Independence Retire Early) goals.

How to Increase Your Savings Rate

Try cutting recurring expenses, automating savings, and increasing income through side hustles or career growth.
