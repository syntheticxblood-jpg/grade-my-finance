How Much Should You Have Saved by 30?

A common rule of thumb says having around one year of salary saved by age 30 is a solid target. It’s only a rule of thumb; real life varies by income, cost of living, and goals.

Why this benchmark exists

Early savings benefit most from compounding. Hitting 1× income by 30 reduces the pressure later, especially if you increase contributions each year.

What actually matters

  • Your savings rate versus your income
  • High-interest debt paid down
  • Emergency fund of at least a few months
